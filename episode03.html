— WHAT HAPPENED —
BEN

Okay so — September 11th, 2001. Al-Qaeda hijacked four commercial planes in the US. Two were flown into the Twin Towers in New York, one hit the Pentagon in Washington, and the fourth — the plane later known as Flight 93 — crashed into a field in Pennsylvania after the passengers actually tried to fight back against the hijackers.

CARL

That last one always gets me. Like, those people knew what was happening and still tried to do something. That's insane.

ILAY

Right? And it's one of the reasons the whole day feels so heavy. Like every part of it has this human weight to it.

BEN

Around three thousand people died that day. And that number actually keeps growing when you include first responders who've developed serious illnesses since — from the dust and debris at Ground Zero.

CARL

I didn't know that. So people are still dying from it now?

BEN

Yeah, unfortunately. There's been a whole legal fight over healthcare coverage for those people. It's been going on for years.

ILAY

And then globally — within weeks the US was in Afghanistan. Airport security changed forever, surveillance laws changed. It basically set the direction for the next two decades of world politics.

CARL

It's wild how one day can just... rewrite everything.

— WHY CONSPIRACY THEORIES EXIST —
CARL

Okay so — why does something like this turn into a conspiracy theory? Like what's the starting point for that?

BEN

So psychologically — and there's actually research on this — humans are always looking for patterns and explanations. When something massive and chaotic happens, our brains want a clean answer. A clear cause. And "a group of terrorists did this" almost feels too simple for something that big.

CARL

Like the scale doesn't match the explanation?

BEN

Exactly. If something is this huge, people feel like the cause must be equally huge. A secret plan, a hidden group pulling strings — that feels more proportionate somehow.

ILAY

And there's also just a general distrust of governments. Which — honestly — isn't always unfair. Governments have lied about things before. That's documented. So when officials come out and say "here's exactly what happened," some people are just not going to take that at face value.

CARL

I mean, I get that. Like if someone has lied to you before, you're not gonna automatically believe them next time.

ILAY

And then the internet exploded this. There was this documentary called "Loose Change" — came out in 2005 — and it went absolutely viral. Millions of views, getting passed around on early YouTube.

CARL

Have you guys ever actually gone down one of those rabbit holes? Like at 2am, one video leads to another...

ILAY

(laughs) Oh absolutely. More than once.

BEN

Yeah it's genuinely hard to stop watching. Even when you know it's questionable.

— TOWER COLLAPSE THEORY —
ILAY

Okay let's get into the actual theories. First one — controlled demolition. The idea that the towers had explosives planted inside and were deliberately brought down.

CARL

Okay wait, before you explain — where does this even come from? Like what made people think that in the first place?

ILAY

Mostly the way the towers fell. Straight down, very symmetrical. And there were people who said they heard what sounded like explosions before the collapse.

CARL

Okay honestly? If I didn't know anything and someone showed me that footage, I could see why it looks weird.

BEN

Yeah and that's the thing — visually it's convincing. But the actual engineering explanation is pretty solid. The planes hit at extremely high speed, and the fires burned hot enough to weaken the steel beams holding up each floor. Once one floor gives way, it crashes into the one below — like a chain reaction. Dominoes, basically.

CARL

So the "straight down" thing isn't suspicious, it's just... physics?

BEN

Exactly. And the logistics of actually planting explosives in the Twin Towers — these were massive, fully occupied buildings. You'd need a huge crew, weeks of work, and somehow nobody notices or talks?

CARL

Yeah that's like — keeping a secret between that many people is basically impossible. Someone always talks.

— BUILDING 7 —
ILAY

Building 7. And this is the one that — even I find hard to fully shake off.

CARL

What's Building 7?

BEN

World Trade Center 7. It was a 47-story building right next to the Twin Towers. It was not hit by a plane. But it caught fire from falling debris, burned for several hours, and then collapsed completely at around 5:20 in the afternoon.

CARL

Wait — it just... fell? By itself?

ILAY

Straight down. Looks almost identical to the towers collapsing. And that's what people can't get past — if no plane hit it, why does it fall the exact same way?

BEN

A US government science agency did a years-long investigation into exactly this. Their conclusion was that fires got completely out of control — the building didn't have a sprinkler system that could handle it — and one key support column failed, which then caused the whole structure to go.

CARL

Still though — of all the things we're talking about today, Building 7 is the one I'd genuinely want an expert to walk me through. Like in person, with diagrams or something.

— PENTAGON MISSILE THEORY —
BEN

Alright — the Pentagon theory. This one says what hit the Pentagon wasn't a plane at all, but a missile or a drone. The main argument is that the hole in the building looked too small for a full commercial aircraft.

CARL

Okay, I've actually heard this one before. My uncle brings this up every Christmas.

ILAY

(laughs) Every family has that one person.

BEN

Physical evidence is pretty clear. Debris from American Airlines Flight 77 was found at the scene. Passengers and crew were identified through DNA. And there were dozens of eyewitnesses on the highway right next to the Pentagon who saw the plane approaching.

CARL

I'm gonna have something to say at Christmas this year.

— INSIDE JOB —
ILAY

Okay. The big one. The theory that the US government either knew about the attacks beforehand and let them happen — or was actually involved in planning them.

CARL

That's... a massive claim. Like if that were true, that would be the biggest scandal in history.

ILAY

And here's where it gets genuinely complicated — because there were real intelligence warnings before the attacks. There's an actual declassified memo from August 2001 with the title "Bin Laden Determined to Strike in US."

CARL

That's a real document?

BEN

Completely real. You can find it online. The 9/11 Commission found serious intelligence failures — agencies not talking to each other, warnings not taken seriously enough. But no evidence of the government planning or helping the attacks happen.

CARL

If someone tells you "we knew something was coming and dropped the ball" — that's almost as hard to accept as the alternative.
